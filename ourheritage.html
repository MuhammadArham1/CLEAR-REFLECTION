Our Heritage

With a history dating back to 1837 we are passionate about our heritage and the legacy of the skilled framemakers of the past that informs our present workmanship

Traditional Frame Makers Since 1837

Originally operating as ‘The Bloomsbury Frameworks’ our history dates back to 1837, at the forefront of avant-garde frame making and supplied finely decorated frames to many leading public and private collections.

The company specialised in the production and application of ‘composition ornament’, then in its early stages, for frames, mirrors and associated furniture.

Home To A National Treasure

The Binning family established their framing company in 1837. It would later become known as the ‘Bloomsbury Frameworks’.

The collection of fine reverse-carved moulds, at the heart of the company, was continuously added to over time to create an extensive and culturally significant library. It includes beautiful running patterns, scrolls, crests, swags, armorials and figures, with some dating back to c1760.

Time honoured techniques, craftsmanship and expertise were passed from father to son, spanning three centuries. The unique skills, acquired over generations to create exceptional frames, became synonymous with the company. The last family owner was Peter Binning (1936-2005).

The company passed to Joseph McCarthy in 2005.
